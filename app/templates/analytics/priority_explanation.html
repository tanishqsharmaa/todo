How Priority Score is Calculated

The AI uses a weighted scoring system to calculate task priority. Each factor contributes to the final 0-100 score:

1. Deadline Urgency (30%)

How close is the due date? Overdue tasks get maximum urgency (80-100). Tasks due today score 95, tomorrow 85, within 3 days 70, within a week 55. No due date defaults to 40.

2. User Importance (25%)

Your assigned importance level (1-5) is directly mapped to a score. Critical (5) = 100, High (4) = 80, Medium (3) = 60, Low (2) = 40, Very Low (1) = 20.

3. Postponement History (20%)

Tasks you've postponed multiple times get higher priority. This helps surface tasks you might be avoiding. Each postponement adds 15 points to this factor.

4. Estimation Accuracy (15%)

Based on your history of actual vs estimated time. If you typically underestimate (tasks take longer than planned), priority is boosted to give you more buffer time.

5. Task Age (10%)

Older pending tasks get a slight priority boost. Tasks older than 14 days score 80, 7+ days score 65, 3+ days score 55, fresh tasks score 40.

Final Score

All weighted factors are combined and normalized to 0-100. High priority: 70+ | Medium priority: 40-69 | Low priority: <40

AI Transparency Note

This priority score is calculated using a rule-based weighted scoring system, not machine learning. Every decision factor is transparent and explainable. The weights and thresholds are configurable and can be replaced with ML models in the future.

The goal is to help you focus on what matters most based on objective criteria, while being completely transparent about how that decision is made.
